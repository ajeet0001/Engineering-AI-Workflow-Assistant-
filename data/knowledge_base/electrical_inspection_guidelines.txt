TITLE: Electrical Systems Inspection and Safety Guidelines
CATEGORY: Electrical Engineering
VERSION: 2.2
LAST UPDATED: 2024-03

PURPOSE:
Define inspection, testing, and safety procedures for electrical systems
including power distribution, motors, control panels, and instrumentation.

SCOPE:
All electrical installations rated up to 33kV in industrial facilities.

1. ELECTRICAL INSPECTION STANDARDS

Pre-Inspection Safety Requirements:
  - Follow Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147.
  - Verify circuit is de-energized using calibrated voltage tester.
  - Establish arc flash exclusion boundary per NFPA 70E.
  - Wear appropriate PPE: arc flash suit, gloves, face shield (rated for incident energy).
  - Never work on live equipment without hot work permit and second person.

1.1 Visual Inspection
  - Check for overheating evidence: discoloration, melted insulation, burn marks.
  - Inspect cable insulation for cracking, abrasion, or moisture damage.
  - Verify all conduit entries are sealed (prevent pest ingress and moisture).
  - Check terminal connections for corrosion or looseness.
  - Verify cable support and tray integrity.
  - Inspect switchgear for dust accumulation (fire and flashover risk).

1.2 Thermographic Survey
  - Conduct under normal load conditions (minimum 40% rated load).
  - Temperature classification:
    - ΔT < 5°C above ambient: NORMAL — monitor.
    - ΔT 5-15°C above ambient: INVESTIGATE — schedule repair.
    - ΔT 15-40°C above ambient: ABNORMAL — repair at next scheduled outage.
    - ΔT > 40°C above ambient: CRITICAL — repair IMMEDIATELY. De-energize if possible.
  - Common hotspots: loose connections, overloaded conductors, failing components.

2. MOTOR TESTING

2.1 Insulation Resistance Testing
  - Use 500V DC Megger for motors <1kV, 1000V DC for motors 1-6.6kV.
  - Acceptance criteria: >1 MΩ (minimum), >100 MΩ recommended for reliable operation.
  - Temperature correct to 40°C baseline using: R40 = Rtest × 0.5^((Ttest-40)/10)
  - If <1 MΩ: Motor must not be energized. Investigate cause (moisture, contamination, insulation breakdown).

2.2 Polarization Index (PI)
  - PI = (10-minute reading) / (1-minute reading)
  - PI > 4: Excellent insulation
  - PI 2-4: Good
  - PI < 2: Insulation is suspect, further investigation required
  - PI < 1: DO NOT ENERGIZE. Insulation failure imminent.

2.3 Motor Current Analysis
  - Measure all three phase currents under full load.
  - Phase current imbalance > 2%: Investigate power supply and connections.
  - Phase current imbalance > 5%: Significant problem, reduce load, investigate.
  - Overall current significantly above nameplate: Overloaded, investigate mechanical load.

3. TRANSFORMER INSPECTION

3.1 Oil-Filled Transformers
  - Check oil level in conservator tank.
  - Sample oil annually for dissolved gas analysis (DGA).
  - DGA key gases (IEEE C57.104 guidelines):
    - Hydrogen (H2): Partial discharge or arcing.
    - Acetylene (C2H2): Arcing — very serious, indicates internal arc.
    - Methane/Ethane: Thermal faults, overheating.
    - Carbon monoxide/dioxide: Paper insulation degradation.
  - Action levels: Refer to IEEE C57.104 Table 1 for gas concentration limits.

3.2 Dry-Type Transformers
  - Check for dust/contamination on windings (fire risk).
  - Inspect for overheating indicators on windings.
  - Verify ventilation openings are clear.
  - Thermal imaging of connections and windings under load.

4. PROTECTION RELAY TESTING

  Overcurrent Protection:
    - Test pick-up current setting: Must operate within ±5% of setpoint.
    - Test time-current characteristic at 200% and 500% of pick-up.
    - Verify instantaneous element operates at set pick-up level.
    - Test reset time.

  Differential Protection:
    - Verify CT ratios and connections.
    - Test restraint and operate characteristics.
    - Apply through-fault current and verify relay restrains.
    - Apply internal fault current and verify relay operates.

  Earth Fault Protection:
    - Verify residual current circuit is complete.
    - Test earth fault relay pick-up and operating time.

5. COMMON ELECTRICAL FAILURES AND CAUSES

Connection Overheating:
  Cause: Loose connection, corrosion, or inadequate torque.
  Corrective Action: Clean, torque to specification, re-thermograph.
  Prevention: Apply calibrated torque wrench, use anti-oxidant compound on aluminum.

Insulation Failure:
  Cause: Age, thermal stress, mechanical damage, moisture ingress, overvoltage.
  Corrective Action: Identify and remove source of degradation. Rewind or replace motor.
  Prevention: Regular Megger testing, maintain dry environment, protect from mechanical damage.

Phase Imbalance:
  Cause: Unbalanced load distribution, loose or corroded connection, blown fuse.
  Corrective Action: Balance loads, repair connections, replace fuse.
  Effect: Increases motor heating by (% imbalance)^2 × 2%. Can cause premature failure.

Harmonic Distortion:
  Cause: Variable frequency drives (VFDs), non-linear loads.
  Effects: Overheating of neutral conductors, transformers, and capacitors.
  Mitigation: Install harmonic filters, use K-rated transformers, properly size neutrals.

6. CABLE TESTING

  High Potential (Hi-Pot) Test:
    - DC Hi-Pot: Apply 80% of factory test voltage for acceptance.
    - For XLPE cables: Follow IEC 60502 for test voltage and duration.
    - Monitor leakage current — rising current indicates insulation degradation.
    - Never apply excessive voltage: Can damage healthy cables.

  Time Domain Reflectometry (TDR):
    - Locate cable faults (open, short, impedance change) precisely.
    - Provides distance to fault in meters.
