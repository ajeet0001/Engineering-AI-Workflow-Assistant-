TITLE: Preventive Maintenance Guide — Rotating Equipment
CATEGORY: Maintenance
VERSION: 3.1
LAST UPDATED: 2024-02

PURPOSE:
Provide a comprehensive preventive maintenance schedule and corrective
maintenance procedures for rotating equipment including pumps, compressors,
motors, and gearboxes.

SCOPE:
All rotating equipment in production facilities rated above 5kW.

1. MAINTENANCE INTERVALS

Daily Checks:
  - Verify lubrication oil levels (±10% of sight glass midpoint).
  - Check for unusual vibration, noise, or heat.
  - Inspect seal areas for leakage.
  - Review temperature readings; alert if bearing temperature exceeds 80°C.
  - Verify all guards are in place.

Weekly Checks:
  - Measure and record vibration levels (velocity, mm/s RMS).
  - Alert threshold: >4.5 mm/s RMS (ISO 10816 Zone B/C boundary).
  - Shutdown threshold: >7.1 mm/s RMS (ISO 10816 Zone D).
  - Inspect flexible couplings for wear or cracking.
  - Check alignment indicators if installed.

Monthly Checks:
  - Lubrication sample analysis for contamination and viscosity.
  - Inspect impeller or rotor for erosion or cavitation damage.
  - Check mechanical seals for wear.
  - Verify motor insulation resistance (>1 MΩ is acceptable).

Quarterly Checks:
  - Full vibration spectrum analysis (FFT).
  - Thermographic survey of motor and bearing housings.
  - Alignment check using laser alignment tool.
  - Replace air filters on motor enclosures.

Annual Overhaul:
  - Full disassembly and inspection.
  - Replace all bearings regardless of condition (life-cycle replacement).
  - Replace mechanical seals.
  - Inspect shaft for wear, scoring, or fatigue cracking.
  - Rebalance rotor to ISO G2.5 or better.
  - Inspect casing for corrosion or erosion.

2. COMMON FAILURE MODES AND CORRECTIVE ACTIONS

Bearing Failure:
  Symptoms: Elevated temperature, high vibration, noise (grinding/whining)
  Causes:
    - Overload
    - Contaminated lubrication
    - Improper installation (fretting)
    - Fatigue
  Corrective Actions:
    - Replace bearing using proper tools (hydraulic press, not hammer).
    - Investigate root cause before returning to service.
    - Check lubrication system for contamination.
    - Verify alignment and balance after replacement.

Seal Failure:
  Symptoms: Leakage at seal face, elevated process fluid in bearing housing
  Causes:
    - Dry running
    - Thermal shock
    - Excessive shaft deflection
    - Incorrect seal face flush
  Corrective Actions:
    - Check seal flush plan and flush fluid quality.
    - Verify shaft runout < 0.05mm TIR.
    - Check for cavitation which causes axial shaft movement.
    - Replace seal with correct specification for process fluid.

Vibration — Unbalance:
  Symptoms: 1x RPM dominant frequency in vibration spectrum
  Corrective Action:
    - Dynamic balance rotor in-situ or in workshop.
    - Target ISO G1.0 for precision equipment, G2.5 for standard.
    - Inspect for erosion or material buildup on rotor.

Vibration — Misalignment:
  Symptoms: 1x and 2x RPM frequencies, high axial vibration
  Corrective Action:
    - Perform laser alignment.
    - Angular and parallel tolerance: <0.05mm/100mm and <0.1mm offset.
    - Check for soft foot before alignment.
    - Verify baseplate is grouted and not deteriorated.

Cavitation:
  Symptoms: Noise (like gravel in pump), pitting on impeller, vibration
  Causes:
    - Insufficient NPSHa (Net Positive Suction Head available)
    - High suction lift, blocked suction strainer
    - Operating far from BEP (Best Efficiency Point)
  Corrective Actions:
    - Clean suction strainer.
    - Reduce flow or increase suction head.
    - Reduce fluid temperature.
    - Consider inducer or impeller change.

3. LUBRICATION GUIDE

Oil-lubricated bearings:
  - Use ISO VG 68 for standard applications.
  - Use ISO VG 100 for high-temperature (>80°C ambient) applications.
  - Change interval: Every 2,000 hours or annually (whichever first).
  - If contamination found, change immediately and investigate source.

Grease-lubricated bearings:
  - Use NLGI Grade 2 lithium complex grease.
  - Re-grease interval: Every 500 hours for standard, every 250 hours for dirty environments.
  - Do not over-grease: Excess grease causes heat buildup.

4. VIBRATION SEVERITY CRITERIA (ISO 10816-3)
  Zone A: <2.3 mm/s — New equipment, acceptable
  Zone B: 2.3-4.5 mm/s — Alert, monitor closely
  Zone C: 4.5-7.1 mm/s — Restricted operation, plan shutdown
  Zone D: >7.1 mm/s — SHUT DOWN IMMEDIATELY

REFERENCES:
- ISO 10816 Mechanical Vibration
- ISO 13709 Centrifugal Pumps
- API 610 Centrifugal Pumps for Petroleum
- ANSI/AGMA 9005 Industrial Gear Lubrication
