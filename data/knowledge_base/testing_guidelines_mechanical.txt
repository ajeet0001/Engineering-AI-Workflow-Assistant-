TITLE: Engineering Testing Guidelines — Mechanical Component Qualification
CATEGORY: Testing Standards
VERSION: 1.8
LAST UPDATED: 2024-03

PURPOSE:
Define the testing protocols and acceptance criteria for qualifying mechanical
components before integration into production systems.

SCOPE:
Covers fatigue testing, tensile testing, hardness testing, and environmental
stress testing for all mechanical components used in critical applications.

1. TENSILE TESTING
Protocol:
  - Sample preparation per ASTM E8/E8M.
  - Test at ambient temperature (23°C ± 5°C) unless otherwise specified.
  - Load application rate: 1-10 mm/min crosshead speed.
  - Record: Yield strength, Ultimate Tensile Strength (UTS), elongation at break.

Acceptance Criteria:
  - Yield strength: Must meet or exceed minimum design value per material specification.
  - UTS must be at least 1.25x the yield strength (minimum ductility requirement).
  - Elongation at break: Minimum 12% for structural steels, 8% for high-strength alloys.

Failure Indicators:
  - Brittle fracture mode (no necking, angular fracture surface) — indicates hydrogen
    embrittlement or heat treatment defects. Reject entire batch, quarantine material.
  - Yield strength below specification — possible incorrect material or heat treatment.
  - Premature failure (below 80% of expected UTS) — inspect for pre-existing defects.

2. FATIGUE TESTING
Protocol:
  - Test per ASTM E466 (constant amplitude axial fatigue).
  - Apply cyclic loading at 70% and 50% of UTS until failure or 10^7 cycles.
  - Record S-N curve data.
  - Test minimum 5 specimens per load level.

Acceptance Criteria:
  - Endurance limit must exceed 40% of UTS for steels.
  - No fatigue failure before 10^6 cycles at 50% UTS load.

Root Cause of Fatigue Failures:
  - Surface defects (scratches, pits) — reduce fatigue life by 30-50%.
  - Residual tensile stresses — from machining or welding without stress relief.
  - Sharp stress concentrations — fillets with radius < 0.5mm are high-risk zones.
  - Corrective action: Shot peening, surface finishing, fillet radius increase.

3. HARDNESS TESTING
Protocol:
  - Rockwell, Vickers, or Brinell per ASTM E18, E384, or E10.
  - Minimum 5 measurements per component; average reported.

Acceptance Criteria:
  - Hardness must fall within ±10% of specified value.
  - No single measurement may deviate >15% from specification.
  - High hardness (>350 HV) with low toughness indicates risk of brittle fracture.

4. ENVIRONMENTAL STRESS TESTING
Salt Spray (Corrosion) Test:
  - Per ASTM B117: 500 hours minimum for outdoor-rated components.
  - Acceptance: No base metal corrosion, no coating delamination >5mm from scribe line.

Thermal Cycling:
  - Range: -40°C to +120°C (or per application specification).
  - 50 cycles minimum; inspect for cracking, delamination, or dimensional change.

5. FAILURE ANALYSIS PROCEDURE
When a component fails testing:
  Step 1: Quarantine all components from the same production batch.
  Step 2: Perform fractographic analysis to determine failure mode.
  Step 3: Review material certifications (Mill Test Reports).
  Step 4: Check manufacturing process parameters (heat treatment, machining).
  Step 5: Issue Non-Conformance Report (NCR) and notify supply chain.
  Step 6: Determine if design modification is required.

REFERENCES:
- ASTM E8/E8M Tensile Testing of Metallic Materials
- ASTM E466 Fatigue Testing
- ISO 6892-1 Metallic Materials Tensile Testing
- ASTM B117 Salt Spray Testing
