TITLE: Standard Operating Procedure — Structural Inspection SOP-ENG-001
CATEGORY: Inspection & Testing
VERSION: 2.3
LAST UPDATED: 2024-01

PURPOSE:
This SOP defines the standard procedure for conducting structural integrity inspections
on mechanical components, pressure vessels, and civil infrastructure.

SCOPE:
Applies to all engineering inspection activities performed in manufacturing plants,
field sites, and maintenance facilities.

PRE-INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS:
1. Verify all personnel have completed relevant safety training (OSHA 10 or equivalent).
2. Ensure calibration certificates for all inspection instruments are current (within 12 months).
3. Obtain written work permit for confined spaces or elevated work areas.
4. Establish an exclusion zone around inspection area per safety guidelines.
5. Review the previous inspection report and any outstanding NCRs (Non-Conformance Reports).

INSPECTION PROCEDURE:
Step 1 — Visual Inspection
  - Examine all accessible surfaces for cracks, corrosion, deformation, or discoloration.
  - Document defects with photographs and GPS coordinates where applicable.
  - Use illuminated magnifier for surface crack detection on welds.

Step 2 — Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)
  - Ultrasonic Testing (UT): Apply to detect sub-surface cracks and wall thickness measurement.
  - Magnetic Particle Inspection (MPI): Apply to ferromagnetic materials for surface/near-surface flaws.
  - Dye Penetrant Inspection (DPI): Apply to non-magnetic materials for surface defects.
  - Radiographic Testing (RT): Apply only when other methods are inconclusive.

Step 3 — Measurement and Documentation
  - Record all dimensional measurements against design tolerances.
  - Note any deformation exceeding 0.5% of nominal dimension as a CRITICAL finding.
  - Document corrosion depth; alert if wall thickness is below 80% of minimum design thickness.

Step 4 — Load Testing (where applicable)
  - Apply proof load at 110% of design working load.
  - Hold for minimum 30 minutes.
  - Monitor for deflection, noise, or instability.

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA:
- No cracks exceeding 2mm in length in load-bearing components.
- Corrosion pitting depth must not exceed 10% of wall thickness.
- Deflection under proof load must not exceed L/300 (where L = span length).
- No evidence of fatigue cracking at stress concentration points.

REJECTION CRITERIA (IMMEDIATE SHUTDOWN):
- Any through-crack discovered in pressure-containing components.
- Wall thickness below 70% of minimum design value.
- Weld failure under proof load.
- Evidence of hydrogen embrittlement or stress corrosion cracking.

POST-INSPECTION:
- Submit completed inspection report within 48 hours.
- Issue NCR for any finding exceeding acceptance criteria.
- Schedule re-inspection interval based on findings (3 months if CRITICAL, 12 months if PASS).

REFERENCES:
- ASME B31.3 Process Piping Standard
- AWS D1.1 Structural Welding Code
- ISO 9712 Non-Destructive Testing Personnel Qualification
