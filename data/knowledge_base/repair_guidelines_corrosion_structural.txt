TITLE: Repair and Remediation Guidelines — Corrosion and Structural Defects
CATEGORY: Repair Instructions
VERSION: 1.5
LAST UPDATED: 2024-02

PURPOSE:
Provide step-by-step repair procedures for common engineering defects including
corrosion, fatigue cracking, weld defects, and structural deformation.

SCOPE:
Applicable to steel structures, pressure piping, mechanical components,
and civil infrastructure elements.

1. CORROSION ASSESSMENT AND REPAIR

1.1 Corrosion Severity Classification
  Class 1 (Surface/Cosmetic):
    - Rust staining only, no measurable metal loss.
    - Wall thickness within specification.
    - Repair: Clean and re-coat. No structural concern.

  Class 2 (Moderate):
    - Metal loss 10-25% of nominal wall thickness.
    - Localized pitting with depth <25% of wall.
    - Repair: Remove affected material, apply compatible filler/patch, re-coat.

  Class 3 (Severe):
    - Metal loss >25% of nominal wall thickness.
    - Through-pitting or localized corrosion threatening structural integrity.
    - Repair: Section replacement required. No patching. Engineering sign-off mandatory.

  Class 4 (Critical):
    - Through-wall corrosion or imminent failure.
    - Action: IMMEDIATE SHUTDOWN. Isolate system. Replace section. Full engineering review.

1.2 Surface Preparation for Repair
  - Blast clean to Sa 2.5 (ISO 8501-1) minimum for all corrosion repair areas.
  - Apply primer coat within 4 hours of blast cleaning (before flash rust).
  - Coating system selection per service environment (immersed, buried, atmospheric).

1.3 Patch Repair Procedure (Class 2 only)
  Prerequisites: Engineering approval required.
  Step 1: Define repair limits — minimum 50mm beyond defect boundary.
  Step 2: Remove all corroded material by grinding.
  Step 3: NDT (UT) to verify sound metal at repair boundary.
  Step 4: Apply weld overlay or insert patch plate.
  Step 5: Post-weld heat treatment if required by material grade and thickness.
  Step 6: NDT the completed repair (UT and MPI).
  Step 7: Apply protective coating system.
  Step 8: Document and update inspection register.

2. FATIGUE CRACK REPAIR

2.1 Crack Assessment
  Before any repair:
  - Determine crack extent using NDT (UT, MPI, or DPI).
  - Map crack length, depth, and orientation.
  - Assess proximity to stress concentration points.
  - Engineering analysis required if crack length >50mm.

2.2 Weld Repair Procedure
  Step 1: Stop drill at crack tips (5mm hole diameter, centered on crack tip).
  Step 2: Grind out crack to clean metal using a carbide burr — verify by DPI.
  Step 3: Preheat per WPS (Weld Procedure Specification) for base material.
  Step 4: Fill with qualified welder using approved WPS.
  Step 5: Post-weld grind flush and profile smooth.
  Step 6: Post-weld heat treatment per code (stress relief at 600-650°C for carbon steel).
  Step 7: NDT repair area: MPI and UT required.
  Step 8: Address root cause to prevent recurrence (redesign, load reduction, etc.).

2.3 Fatigue Crack Root Causes
  - Cyclic loading exceeding endurance limit.
  - Stress concentration at geometry change (notch, hole, abrupt section change).
  - Residual tensile stresses from welding.
  - Surface damage initiating crack propagation.
  Prevention:
  - Shot peen repaired area to introduce compressive surface residual stress.
  - Increase fillet radius at stress concentrations.
  - Reduce cyclic load amplitude through vibration isolation or load redistribution.

3. WELD DEFECT REPAIR

Common Weld Defects and Repairs:
  Porosity:
    - Cause: Moisture contamination, shielding gas issues, base metal contamination.
    - Repair: Grind out porous area, re-weld, re-inspect.
    - Prevention: Preheat, use dry electrodes, clean base metal.

  Incomplete Fusion:
    - Cause: Insufficient heat input, incorrect technique, wrong electrode angle.
    - Repair: Back-gouge to sound metal, re-weld.
    - Detection: UT or RT required (not visible by MPI).

  Cracks (Hot or Cold):
    - Hot cracks: Solidification cracks in weld metal, often longitudinal.
    - Cold cracks (HAZ): Hydrogen-induced, appear within 48 hours of welding.
    - Repair: Remove crack completely (stop drill + grind), repair per Section 2.2.
    - Prevention: Post-weld hydrogen bake-out (200°C for 4 hours) for susceptible materials.

  Undercut:
    - Cause: Excessive heat, high travel speed, incorrect electrode angle.
    - Acceptable limit: 0.8mm depth for structural, 0.4mm for fatigue-loaded joints.
    - Repair: Grind smooth if within limit; weld fill if exceeds limit.

4. STRUCTURAL DEFORMATION REPAIR

  Distortion Assessment:
    - Measure deviation from design geometry.
    - Tolerance: Typically L/500 for structural members (L = member length).
    - Exceed tolerance: Engineering analysis required before repair.

  Cold Straightening:
    - Apply only when deformation is <2% strain.
    - Use hydraulic jacks with controlled force.
    - Monitor with dial gauges.
    - Re-inspect welds in deformed area after straightening.

  Hot Straightening:
    - Heat to 600-650°C (cherry red) for carbon steel.
    - Never exceed 750°C (risks grain growth and strength reduction).
    - Allow to cool slowly; do not quench.
    - Verify hardness and tensile properties after hot straightening.

5. REPAIR DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
  All repairs must include:
  - Pre-repair NDT records showing defect extent.
  - Repair procedure reference (approved WPS, repair drawing).
  - Welder qualification records.
  - Post-repair NDT records.
  - Engineering sign-off.
  - Updated inspection register entry with next inspection date.
